Scientifically Proven

A study published in the journal Science Advances has extensively investigated the influence of the N1 stage of sleep on creativity and problem-solving abilities. The research focused on the phenomenon known as the 'Eureka moment', a term derived from the Greek word meaning "I have found it," which refers to the instant in the cognitive process where individuals suddenly experience a significant insight. Eureka moment occurs in N1 sleep stage. N1 stage often marked by a moment of relaxation, such as the relaxing of finger muscles.

The study revealed two primary findings:

  1. Subjects exhibited improved problem-solving performance following the N1 sleep stage.
  2. There was a notable difference in the time taken to solve problems before and after reaching the Eureka moment.

Used by Creative Masters

Thomas Edison

Thomas Edison, known for holding 1,093 patents[3], enhanced his creativity using the hypnagogic state, a semi-conscious phase between sleep and wakefulness. Preferring short sleep, he napped with balls in his hands, dropping them as he dozed off, a method to capture creative thoughts at peak moments. This technique significantly boosted his inventive output, contributing to his legacy as a pioneer in innovation and productivity[4].

Salvador Dalí

Salvador Dalí boosted his creativity with the "Dalí Sleep Method," a technique where he held a key over a metal plate while dozing in a chair, awakening himself as he began to sleep when the key dropped. This method allowed him to access dream-like, subconscious thoughts. It significantly influenced his productivity: Dalí produced at least 1,178 paintings[1], with some sources citing over 1,600[2].
